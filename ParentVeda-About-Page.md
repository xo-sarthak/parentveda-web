## What the About page is for

Two jobs:
1. **Deepen trust** — someone clicked "About" because they want to know *can I trust these people with one of the most tender times of my life?* Answer that with mission, values, and how we handle credibility — not hype.
2. **Reinforce the difference** — calm vs clinical, rooted vs Western, bilingual vs English-only. This is also an SEO page (brand + "about ParentVeda" queries), so it should be real prose, not a thin stub.

Keep it the same calm, bilingual voice as everywhere else. *A calm nursery, never a toy store.*

---

## Section-by-section outline (with draft copy)

### 1. Hero / lead — reuse the landing "About" copy
Open with the section you already love, so the page and the landing feel of a piece:

> **Most pregnancy apps feel clinical. ParentVeda feels like home.**
> So many apps are clinical, Western and quietly anxious — full of scores, alerts and worry. ParentVeda is different. It's calm by design, culturally rooted, and speaks your language: English and Hinglish, side by side.

### 2. Our mission / why we exist
A few honest lines on the "why":

> **We believe pregnancy in India deserves its own kind of companion.**
> One that honours dadi-maa ke nuskhe and Garbh Sanskar as much as modern, evidence-informed guidance — and never trades your calm for your attention. We're building the gentle, rooted companion we wished existed: warmth and wisdom, every single week, in the language you actually speak at home.

### 3. What we believe (values)
Expand the landing "promise" into a short values list, each with a line:
- **Calm over clicks** — no scores, no streaks, no fear-language. Ever.
- **Rooted, not borrowed** — Indian tradition at the centre, not as a sprinkle on top.
- **Bilingual, always** — English and Hinglish, side by side, from day one.
- **Gentle, not clinical** — a calm companion, never a dashboard.
- **Honest about tradition** — we celebrate nuskhe and rituals, and we're truthful about what the evidence says.
- **Privacy as calm** — your journey is yours; we never sell your data. *(link → /privacy)*

### 4. What's inside ParentVeda (the substance)
A short paragraph + link to Features, so About isn't all sentiment:

> ParentVeda walks every week from 4 to 40 — a daily calm moment, a swipeable week-by-week journey, Garbh Sanskar's five gentle rituals, a Pregnancy Journey Map, quiet practical tools, a parallel Father Mode, and a warm parenting community. **[See everything inside →]** *(links to Features on `/`)*

### 5. How we handle wisdom & credibility (E-E-A-T — important for a health brand)
This is the trust core that *replaces* a founder bio:

> **Gentle, but grounded.** Our guidance blends timeless Indian wisdom with modern, evidence-informed care. We write in plain language, we cite where it matters, and we're honest about myths — celebrating the tradition while gently telling you what the evidence says. ParentVeda is a calm companion, **not** a substitute for your doctor — always consult your OB-GYN for medical decisions.

*(When you have a medical/practitioner reviewer, add: "Our content is reviewed by [name/credential]." This is the single strongest trust signal for health content — and it isn't a founder bio, so it fits your "no founder details yet" call.)*

### 6. Bilingual commitment (a short, distinctive section)
> **Aapki bhasha, aapka companion.** Pregnancy advice shouldn't feel like a translation. ParentVeda speaks English and Hinglish side by side — so it feels like home, not a textbook.

## Practical notes
- **SEO:** title ~ *"About ParentVeda — a calm, bilingual pregnancy companion"*; meta description echoing the mission. Emit `Organization` + `AboutPage` JSON-LD.
- **Length:** ~400–700 words of real prose — enough to be credible and indexable, not a wall.
- **Tone check:** if a line could appear in a clinical Western app, rewrite it. Watch the repetition of "calm/gentle" — vary with *soothing, unhurried, rooted, tender, warm.*
- **Links out:** Features (`/`), The Journal (`/journal`), Privacy (`/privacy`), Waitlist (`/#waitlist`).
